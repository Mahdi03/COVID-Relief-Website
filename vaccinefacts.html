What is the COVID-19 Vaccine?

The COVID-19 vaccine is an important tool and resource to bring you closer to enjoying the activities you have missed. It will help prevent severe COVID infections and it provides hope for what is to come.

COVID-19 vaccines do NOT alter or interact with our DNA

  There are currently 2 types of vaccines that have been authorized and recommended for use in the United states: messenger RNA (mRNA) vaccines and a viral vector vaccine. Both types of COVID-19 vaccines deliver instructions (genetic material) to our cells to start creating protection against the virus that causes COVID-19. However, the material never enters the nucleus of the cell, where DNA is stored. This means that the genetic material in the vaccines cannot affect or interact with our DNA.

After getting a COVID-19 Vaccine, you will NOT test positive for COVID-19 on a viral test.

  None of the authorized or recommended vaccines cause you to test positive or viral tests which detect if you have a current infection. If your body develops an immune response to vaccination, which is the goal, you may test positive on some antibody tests. Antibody tests indicate that you have had a previous infection and that you may have some level of protection against the virus.

The COVID-19 Vaccine is safe for all who take it and is very effective!

  Let's all do our part to help our communities and businesses, make sure to get your COVID vaccine! Be on the lookout for vaccination areas and events. Always care for others around you and in your community. Make sure to spread positivity and only the facts about the vaccines!
